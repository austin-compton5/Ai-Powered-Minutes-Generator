  • David A: David A provided an update that staff presented the plastic waste reduction regulation ordinance to the City Council, encompassing disposable food ware standards, banning plastic stirrers and utensils, and mandating reusables for dine-in premises. 5. Letter from the Sustainability Commission for City Council's Consideration of New Drive-Through Regulations (Action)

Presented by David A

Summary: The commission wishes to submit a letter to City Council expressing support to revisit a prohibition on new drive-through establishments.

Comments:

  • Commissioner B.: Commissioner B provided more information and background to the commission. He mentioned that after going through deliberation at the last Commission meeting, appropriate edits were made. The letter outlines the request, implications of drive-throughs on transportation, and sustainability initiatives. He said he would be happy to walk through exhibit examples.
  • Commissioner Werner: Commissioner Werner thanked Commissioner B for incorporating the comments made previously, and stated that the photos used demonstrate the effect of backed up traffic and alleyways taken over by private business.
  • Vice Chair Kartoonyan: Vice Chair Kartoonyan noted that one-size-fits-all does not work, as some establishments are busier than others, and some exceed the 500 ft queue, impeding the off ramp from the 134. Also he noted the need to include the caption that the images depict the City of LA.
  • Commissioner B.: Commissioner B acknowledged that a prior suggestion had been made to discuss the traffic implications with Public Works.
  • Nora: Nora related that the council has changed its procedure and now there is no discussion allowed for action items.
  • Chairperson Bazemore: Chairperson Bazemore voiced support for the letter stating I appreciate the work you put in particularly in the beginning for highlighting the carbon, you know and making sure that we point out that transportation emissions are a bulk of our greenhouse gas emissions in Glendale.
