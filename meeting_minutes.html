5. Letter from the Sustainability Commission for City Council's Consideration of New Drive-Through Regulations (Action)

Presented by David Nazarian

Summary: David Nazarian stated that the commission is to submit a letter to the City Council expressing support to revisit a prohibition on new drive-through establishments in the City of Glendale. The letter was presented at the January 13th special meeting, where commission members provided feedback on a draft presented by Commissioner Bartusek. The amended letter is presented for the commission to consider submitting to City Council.

Comments:

  • Commissioner Bartusek: Commissioner Bartusek provided background on the origin and intent of the letter, which was a result of City Council's inability to extend the temporary moratorium on drive-throughs, and the concerns regarding their impact outside of the downtown specific plan area. The letter outlines the implications of drive-throughs on transportation and sustainability initiatives. He detailed changes made to the letter based on requests, including adding specific asks for a moratorium and including exhibits.
  • Commissioner Werner: Commissioner Werner thanked Commissioner Bartusek for incorporating the comments well. Adding the image, is worth a thousand words. It brings to mind how alleys have been taken over, and the city doesn't benefit, and the public doesn't benefit. Enforcement for these types of issues is something to address. Since some are being proposed for South Glendale, her neighborhood, and her neighbors are not happy.
  • Vice Chair Cartunian: The Vice Chair made several remarks. His first point was that one size fits all doesn't work, with the limited car clearance example. His second point was regarding mentioning the City of Los Angeles in the letter as the reason being a limitation of authority, although noting to clarify that the property being discussed is in Glendale, but the impact is felt on Glendale residents crossing over into Los Angeles on the off-ramp from the 134.
  • Commissioner Bartusek: Commissioner Bartusek reminded members that the Foothill and Lowell in-and-out is just outside city limits, also generating traffic within Glendale.
  • Vice Chair Cartunian: Traffic study is needed there. People cross over the yellow lines when not interested in a drive-through. Police officers have seen it. Imploring them to address safety issues.
  • Chairperson Basemore: Chairperson Basemore also indicated having witnessed traffic congestion, stated appreciation for the detail incorporated in the letter, and expressed support for the letter as written.
  • Commissioner Bartusek: He pointed out that they did their due diligence and research to include what was possible, within reason. It's very difficult to address the impact that comes as a result of these establishments once they're approved. The most they could do, realistically, is hope police ticket drivers who are blocking traffic. There is already a pre-application for another in-and-out near Pacific and Verdugo, an area with residential streets nearby. He believed that all has been said.
  • Commissioner Werner: She asks about the potential to revoke the drive-through component of a restaurant if the traffic impact becomes a problem.
  • David Nazarian: David Nazarian stated that staff would need to look at the conditions of approval. A revocation would be due to a pattern of violation of those conditions, in a due process hearing. He would have to think about whether there would be a violation of policies of circulation, and as opposed to a express condition that's attached to permit issuance. So it's something staff would have to look into.
  • Vice Chair Cartunian: Vice Chair Cartunian remarked that he found it hard to believe that the city has no enforcement mechanism. When doing infrastructure upgrades, permits and traffic plans are required to block a lane; in this case, the businesses are using a lane every day without pulling a permit. The city can and should sit down with the establishments and ask them to come up with a solution.
  • Commissioner Bartusek: The area in Grandview and Glenoaks, illustrates the degree to which Public Works is willing to work around a private use of public right of way. There is a specific establishment that is violating the approved circulation plan, however no action has been taken. Approving these establishments makes it very difficult to address the impacts that come as a result.
